How was coffee first discovered?

The legend of coffees discovery dates back to the 11th century, where a farmer found that one of his goats began acting very energetic after eating berries from a 'coffea arabica' tree. The farmer himself tried some of the berries, finding himself to have the same energetic feel. The coffee berries were then took to religious leaders, who (after some convincing) put them into hot water, leading to the drinks discovery.

It wasn't until the 15th century that coffee was widely accepted by society, as it was believed to be a drink made from the devil. However, soon coffee found its way across the world, with England and other countries discovering it in the 17th discovery, gradually transforming it into the drink we all know and love today.
