Let's raise some awareness.

It's been a tough year...

And because of that, the world has seen a huge impact on mental health.

We understand that everyone, from all ages and backgrounds, has had their mental health impacted from the ongoing effects of COVID-19.

So, to try and help this, we're working with Adhar Project, a mental health charity based in Leicester, to try and support some groups who are struggling with their mental health and raise some awareness of the issues they're facing.

Children's Mental Health Week

This year, Children's Mental Health Week is from the 1st February - 7th February. Place2Be, the charity who organised the week, will be raising awareness for Children's Mental Health and finding ways online to get the community involved to help raise awareness.

The theme of Children's Mental Health Week is "Express Yourself", so we're going to be doing our best to encourage everyone, regardless of age or background, to share your thoughts and feelings through your unique creativity, and to do activities that help your mood.

Why do we need to raise awareness?

Studies from July 2020 have found;

  • 1/6 children and young people have a diagnosable mental health condition (this has increased from 1/9 in 2017);

  • 1/2 of those with lifetime mental health issues first experience symptoms by the age of 14.

With the ongoing affects of COVID-19 causing challenges to childrens education, home and social life, these trends are only likely to get worse.

What are we doing to help?


On behalf of Adhar, we're going to take control of their social media pages from the 1st - 7th February 2021 for Children's Mental Health Week.

We'll be using their platforms to spread awareness messages about the current issues that children across the country are experiencing and suggesting ways to solve them, whilst also ensuring that we spread some awareness for the parents looking after these children too.

Finding Support

If your child, or someone you know, is struggling with their mental health in any way and needs support, here's a few places you can find support.


Place2Be

Place to be is an incredible children's mental health charity (they're the ones who are hosting the week). With over 25 years of experience, they've worked with hundreds of schools across the country and helped many children and young people, so it's almost certain that you can find support in your childs school.

Checkout their website by following the link here: Place2Be.

YoungMinds

This is another amazing charity that provide a lot of information for children, young people and parents for mental health. They also provide a lot of helpful resources to talk to someone, no matter what your issues may be.

Follow this link to visit the website for YoungMinds.

Childline

Childline can be contacted about anything, giving you a voice to speak to on the other end of the phone, on 1-2-1 chat or through email. You'll be able to speak to a counsellour about your issues, no matter what it is, and they will find a way to support you in the most effective way possible.

Their website also have anonymous message boards, where young people can seek and offer support on whatever their worries are.

If you wish to visit their website, follow this link: Childline.

Local Services

You can use this NHS service to find local charities and organisations in your area, who are able to offer children and young people mental health support. A great example of a Leicester based charity is Adhar Project, a mental health charity who offer support to people of all backgrounds.

To find your local services for mental health support, go to this NHS site.
